The fundamentals, minus bro-science and fear-mongering.

Eating 101 — why the diet plan works

Calories decide weight; protein decides what that weight is made of. Your plan sets both from your stats (Mifflin-St Jeor + activity). A modest deficit (~400 kcal) loses fat without wrecking energy; a small surplus (~250) builds muscle without excess fat.

Protein target: ~1.8 g per kg bodyweight, split across meals. It keeps you full, protects muscle in a cut, and builds it in a bulk.

Cheap protein that carries every budget plan: eggs, canned tuna, chicken thighs, Greek yogurt, milk, lentils, beans, frozen mince, whey (cheapest per gram of protein there is).

You don't need to be perfect. Hitting calories ±10% and protein ±20 g daily beats a "perfect" plan you quit in week 2.

Skincare 101 — the only routine most people need

Morning: gentle cleanser → moisturizer → SPF 30+. Daily SPF is the single highest-leverage skincare habit that exists.

Evening: cleanser → moisturizer. For acne, adapalene 0.1% three nights a week building to nightly — judge at week 8–12, not week 2. Persistent acne deserves a doctor; prescriptions work.

Three products used daily beat fifteen used randomly.

Training — the honest beginner blueprint

Three full-body sessions a week transforms a beginner inside a year: squat pattern, hinge, push, pull, carry. 2–3 sets of 6–12 reps, add a little weight most weeks. That progressive overload is the entire secret — everything else is decoration.

Can't access a gym? Push-up progressions, backpack rows, split squats, and a doorframe pull-up bar cover 80% of it for the price of a pizza.

Sleep & water — the real cheat codes

7–9 hours of sleep improves skin repair, training recovery, mood, and how you look and feel every single morning. 2 L+ of water, less late-night salt and alcohol = sharper face by breakfast. These are free and they compound.

Style starter pack

Fit beats brand. Palette: navy, grey, white, black, olive. Foundations: dark straight jeans, plain tees that fit the shoulders, one overshirt, clean white sneakers. A tailor's hem is the best money in fashion.

Mindset — why there are no ratings here

Rating apps and blackpill forums profit from making you feel stuck: hopeless users keep scrolling. The evidence runs the other way — grooming, fitness, skin, style, posture, and confidence dominate real-world first impressions, and every one of them is trainable. So this app measures actions, and only actions.

Compare yourself to yesterday's you. Monthly progress photos, not hourly mirror checks. And if appearance thoughts are eating your day or causing real distress, talk to someone — a doctor or therapist, not a rating algorithm. That's not weakness; it's maintenance, and it's what someone who takes their glow-up seriously would do.
